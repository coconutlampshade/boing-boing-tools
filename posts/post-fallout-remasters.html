Fallout 3 and New Vegas supposedly getting Oblivion-style remasters

Not releasing something Fallout: New Vegas-related to tie in with the second season of the immensely popular TV show taking place in that game's setting would be dropping the ball on a truly Biblical level. Then again, Bethesda is no stranger to dropping the ball.

A promotional page for the show features a mysterious locked section hidden behind a countdown — and while that countdown coincides with the season finale, some believe it might also tease a remaster of Fallout 3 or New Vegas.

It's not quite as far-fetched as it sounds. Court documents from the Microsoft/Activision acquisition that accurately leaked the Oblivion remaster also included a directly-named Fallout 3 remaster and a mysterious Project Platinum, which some have taken as a reference to the Platinum Chip, New Vegas' central MacGuffin. Bethesda's Todd Howard has also expressed a desire to continue shadow-dropping games after the Oblivion approach.

A few outlets claim confidential sources have confirmed the remastered duology is coming at some point. Windows Central reports they're "eventually" getting a Fallout 3 remake in the vein of Oblivion Remastered, as well as Fallout New Vegas. They note Obsidian has four new projects — and Fallout co-creator Tim Cain recently joined the studio.

Whether the remasters arrive next month or next year is anyone's guess. I wouldn't put too much stock in that Amazon countdown timer — most likely it's just a behind-the-scenes featurette with spoilers. Given that Oblivion Remastered is nearly twenty times larger than the original, though, start freeing up hard drive space now.
