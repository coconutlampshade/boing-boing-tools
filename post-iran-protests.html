Iran's Supreme Leader threatens protesters as death toll reaches 62

As Iran's economy craters — the rial has collapsed to 1.4 million to the dollar — citizens have taken to the streets in what's become the most significant challenge to the regime in years. The response from the theocracy that has lorded over the country since 1979 has been predictable: threats, violence, and an internet blackout to hide it all.

According to The Globe and Mail, Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei dismissed the protests as attempts to "please the president of the United States," telling demonstrators to pay attention to Trump's problems instead. Meanwhile, Iran's judiciary chief Gholamhossein Mohseni-Ejei promised punishment "will be decisive, maximum and without any legal leniency."

At least 62 people have died. The nationwide internet shutdown that began Thursday makes documenting security force actions nearly impossible — which is, of course, the point. Analysts suggest the blackout provides cover for violent suppression.

The protests began December 28 over soaring inflation and the collapsing currency. Former Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi called for demonstrations Thursday and Friday evenings, helping escalate the movement. Whether the regime's iron fist can crush it remains to be seen, but they're certainly trying.
