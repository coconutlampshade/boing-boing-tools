One Piece live-action returns for more nautical nonsense

I've never actually watched the fantastical pirate-themed anime One Piece, largely due to its imposing length. (Pause to snicker.) I am a little bitter that Netflix actually seems to have hit the mark with its live-action adaptation, though—in stark contrast to its handling of classics like Avatar: The Last Airbender and Cowboy Bebop.

When a second season was announced last year, fan response was massively positive, a sentiment that seems to have persisted through the recent release of an actual trailer. It has everything you could want: swashbuckling, swordfighting, stretching, and a lot of extremely improbable wigs.
