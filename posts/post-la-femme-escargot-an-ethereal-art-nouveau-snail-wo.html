La femme-escargot: an ethereal art nouveau snail-woman sculpture from 1900

This 1900 sculpture depicts an ethereal snail-woman hybrid with a delicate human face emerging from antennae and shell. Called La Femme-Escargot, the figure turns over her shoulder with closed eyes, evoking a dreamlike quality.

From Archaeology & Art on Instagram:

"Snail Woman Figurine Inkwell (aka La Femme-Escargot). Artist: Henry Schmid (French, 1872–1927). Date: c. 1900. Material: Cast bronze with dark brown patina; Height 15 cm. Collection: Private Collection; Christie's auction record.⁣

⁣We're looking at a hybrid being. This snail woman reflects the interspecies metamorphosis aesthetic of the Art Nouveau movement. This visual approach was inspired by the scientific and philosophical developments and ideas of that era.⁣"
