Trump administration freezes $10 billion in social services to five blue states

The Trump administration has halted approximately $10 billion in federal social service funding to five Democratic-led states: Minnesota, New York, California, Illinois, and Colorado. According to CBS News, the freeze affects $7 billion in Temporary Assistance for Needy Families payments, $2.4 billion in child care development funds, and $870 million in social services grants benefiting children.

HHS spokesperson Andrew Nixon justified the move by claiming these states "have been complicit in allowing massive amounts of fraud to occur under their watch." But here's the thing: the administration presented no evidence of fraud schemes in New York, Illinois, or Colorado. For California, Trump posted on Truth Social that "the fraud investigation of California has begun" without offering a single supporting detail.

Democrats accused the administration of weaponizing congressionally approved funding against blue states. As CBS News noted, "Beyond Minnesota, the Trump administration has also not put forth evidence of sweeping fraud schemes in social services in New York, Illinois and Colorado."
