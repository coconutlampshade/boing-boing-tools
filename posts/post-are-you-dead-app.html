"Are you dead?" app goes viral in China with 10 million downloads

An app that asks "Are you dead?" went viral in China and hit the top of Apple's App Store with over 10 million downloads.

The single-purpose app for people who live alone has no bells or whistles. Users enter their name and the email address of a friend or family member — preferably one comfortable with coming over to check for a dead body. The interface is just a white Pac-Man-esque ghost on a green background. Daily, the app asks "Are you dead?" After two missed check-ins, it notifies your emergency contact.

One developer told Wired that after making apps purely for entertainment, they wanted something different. "When I looked at Maslow's hierarchy of needs, I saw that safety needs are deeper and apply to a much broader group of people."

The app debuted last June at 1 RMB (14 cents) but has since risen to 8 RMB ($1.15) amid a social media-fueled surge. No subscription fee. The developers plan to rename it "Demumu," combining the word for death with a play on Labubus, the 2020s answer to Beanie Babies.
