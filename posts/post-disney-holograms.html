Patent filing reveals upcoming Disney theme park holograms

Theme park expert Len Testa revealed on his podcast The Disney Dish that Disney is developing a new holographic system for its ride systems that will appear to put characters closer to your vehicle than ever before.

Testa found a patent application filed by Disney Enterprises Inc. on May 16, 2024, and published on November 20, 2025, for "Holographic Character Creation." The application explains that physical animatronics placed too close to guests can be touched or interfered with — holographic images can appear closer without that danger.

Testa speculates the technology may debut on an Encanto ride currently under construction in Animal Kingdom's Tropical Americas land, planned for 2027. The ride vehicles in the patent diagrams — two cars with two rows of seats each — match what's rumored for the Encanto attraction. The patent even shows an alligator character, which fits Antonio's supernatural ability to communicate with animals.

Testa hints he has inside knowledge: "The turn of the track and placement of the show scene look like the first scene after the ride load area in the upcoming Encanto ride track. Allegedly. Not that we have any inside knowledge on what the track layout looks like. None whatsoever. No one has shown us anything."
