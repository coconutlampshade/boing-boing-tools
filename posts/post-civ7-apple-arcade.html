Civilization 7 is coming to Apple Arcade, with no Android version planned

Civilization 7 is far from my favourite instalment in the long-running, time-sucking 4X strategy series. I had a hard time getting into the way it forces you to start fresh with every new era your civilization enters — it made the game feel like it lacked continuity. But lots of people love it! Enough of them, in fact, for Behaviour Interactive—the studio behind Fallout Shelter—to spend its time porting it to Apple's tablets and smartphones. According to GameSpot, there are currently no plans for an Android port.

The Apple Arcade edition will feature touch-specific controls for on-the-go play. And if it's anything like the excellent port of Civilization 6, it should keep most of the in-game elements from the PC version. The burning question: how long will it be an Apple Arcade exclusive? Plenty of iPhone owners would buy a license outright but have no interest in a monthly Arcade subscription.

It's also worth wondering how many future desktop-class AAA games Apple will confine to their subscription service. As the company moves to powering its portable devices with the same chips as their computers, bringing big-name games to Apple hardware might actually become tempting for studios. With the news this week that Apple is launching a subscription service to counter Adobe's creative suite, I wouldn't put it past Tim Cook and the gang to put the games Apple users want behind a monthly paywall. With hardware sales declining as production costs rise, forcing subscriptions on customers is key to the company making its next trillion bucks.
