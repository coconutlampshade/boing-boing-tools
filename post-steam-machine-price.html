Steam Machine price leaks through Czech storefront

We haven't heard much about Valve's cube-shaped "living room PC" since its announcement last year, presumably because they've been waiting to see whether RAM prices stabilize. One of the biggest concerns has been the price, especially as PC component costs continue to climb.

Thanks to Czech tech retailer smarty.cz, we might have a ballpark estimate. Their Steam Machine listing looks standard at first, but as one cunning Reddit user discovered, inspecting the page's HTML reveals a badly-concealed price field. The 512GB model runs about $950, with the 1TB version at $1,100.

Take this with a grain of salt — retailers often add extra margin, and nothing is official. Even so, these aren't exactly console-competitive prices (not yet, anyway), which raises the question of who exactly the Steam Machine is for. PC gamers hardcore enough to be tempted already know how to cast games to a TV, and console gamers are being asked to make a significant leap in expense.

Half-Life 3 would be one hell of a killer app, though.
