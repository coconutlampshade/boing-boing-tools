Amazon's God of War adaptation has its Kratos

Hey, remember that live-action God of War show Amazon announced more than three years ago? No? I didn't either until just now, but it's been chugging along behind the scenes without our knowledge.

After the Fallout TV show turned out pretty damn good, I'm less leery of future video game adaptations from Amazon Studios. God of War is probably another few years out, but Amazon announced a substantial step: actor Ryan Hurst was cast as Kratos of Sparta.

Hurst played Thor in PlayStation's God of War Ragnarök, bringing established familiarity with the franchise to the role of Kratos. He was nominated for a BAFTA Award for his performance.

Thor is the only role I know Hurst from offhand, but he absolutely crushed it, and familiarity with the series is obviously a plus. While casting the main character is a big deal, the press release included bonus details, some juicier than others.

Lightning round: Wheel of Time's Rafe Judkins is out as showrunner, with Battlestar Galactica's Ronald D. Moore stepping up. Cory Barlog, director of the much-loved 2018 God of War reboot, is serving as executive producer. The entire series seems to adapt that game in particular, according to an official synopsis:

In God of War, the live action series, his story will closely follow the path of the last two games as he deals with something new: his ten year old son Atreus. This father and son dynamic began a whole new chapter in the legendary series and will be the heartbeat of the new show.

Still no word on Atreus' casting. I'm a little disappointed they're adapting a pre-existing story instead of telling a new one in the setting — I hate to point to Fallout again, but that show's approach was an absolute slam dunk. Kratos has been around for centuries; surely he's gotten into some other mischief worthy of being put onscreen.
