From Blondie's bass player to occult author: the two lives of Gary Lachman

In the 1970s, Gary Lachman — then Gary Valentine — played bass for Blondie, wrote the music for their first single "X-Offender," and lived with Debbie Harry and Chris Stein in the band's legendary Bowery loft. His song "(I'm Always Touched by Your) Presence, Dear" became a UK top ten hit.

Then he walked away from rock to write about consciousness and the occult. He's now published over 26 books on subjects ranging from Aleister Crowley to Colin Wilson.

This short film by his son Max Jones-Lachman traces the arc — from comic books at five, to Hermann Hesse as a teenager, to discovering esoteric traditions while living the New York punk life. It's promoting Lachman's new memoir, Touched by the Presence.

"It's about becoming who you are," Lachman says. "About bringing forth what is within you and actualizing your potentials."
